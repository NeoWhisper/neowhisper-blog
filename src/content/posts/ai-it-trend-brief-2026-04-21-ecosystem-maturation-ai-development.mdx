## App Review Guidelines Updated: Anonymous Chat Apps Now Under UGC Rules

Apple has revised the App Review Guidelines to clarify that apps offering random or anonymous chat functionality are now subject to guideline 1.2, which covers User-Generated Content. This change, published February 6, 2026, closes a gap where chat apps could previously operate without the moderation requirements applied to other UGC platforms.

Guideline 1.2 requires apps with user-generated content to include content filtering, reporting mechanisms, and a method for blocking abusive users. By extending this to anonymous and random chat apps, Apple is enforcing a baseline safety standard across social communication features.

For developers, this means any app that pairs users randomly or allows anonymous messaging must now implement:

- Content moderation tools (automated or manual)
- User reporting flows
- Blocking functionality
- Contact information for reporting concerns to the developer

Translations of the updated guidelines will be available on the Apple Developer website within one month of the announcement.

## Alternative Payment Options Arrive on visionOS 1.2 in the EU

Since July 2024, apps distributed on the App Store in the EU can offer alternative payment options on visionOS 1.2. This extends the alternative payment framework that was already available on iOS in the EU to Apple's spatial computing platform.

Developers who choose to use alternative payment providers must still comply with Apple's requirements, including:

- Providing a link to the alternative payment provider within the app
- Continuing to offer Apple's in-app purchase alongside the alternative
- Following the guidelines for alternative payment providers in the EU

The alternative payment option does not eliminate Apple's commission entirely. Developers using alternative payment providers are still subject to a reduced commission rate, as outlined in Apple's terms for the EU Digital Markets Act compliance.

For visionOS developers specifically, this means payment flows must now account for two possible checkout paths: the standard in-app purchase flow and the alternative provider flow. Both paths must function correctly within the spatial computing interface.

## What This Means for Developers

These two updates affect different aspects of app development but share a common theme: Apple is tightening compliance expectations across the platform.

**For chat and social app developers:** The UGC guideline clarification means any app with anonymous or random pairing features must now build moderation infrastructure. This affects not only dedicated chat apps but also any app that includes social features as a secondary function (e.g., community tabs in productivity apps, anonymous feedback features).

- **Moderation tooling:** Implement automated content filtering and a clear reporting pipeline before your next App Store submission.
- **User safety features:** Add blocking and muting functionality that meets guideline 1.2 requirements.
- **Legal review:** Update your app's terms of service to reflect the new moderation policies.

**For visionOS developers in the EU:** The alternative payment option creates new monetization paths but adds implementation complexity.

- **Dual payment paths:** Your app must handle both Apple in-app purchase and alternative provider checkout flows.
- **Commission structure:** Review the reduced commission rates that apply when using alternative payment providers.
- **UI consistency:** Ensure both payment flows work naturally within the spatial computing interface.
